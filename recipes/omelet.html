Omelet

Description

An omelet is a type of egg-based dish made by cooking beaten eggs in a pan. It can be filled with a variety of ingredients, such as cheese, vegetables, and meat, to add flavor and texture. Omelets are a quick and easy breakfast or lunch option, and can be customized to suit your taste preferences.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon milk
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Fillings of your choice (cheese, vegetables, meat, etc.)

Steps

  1. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat them with a fork. Add the milk and salt, and mix until well combined.
  2. Melt the butter in a pan over medium heat. Once the butter is melted and hot, pour the egg mixture into the pan.
  3. As the eggs start to cook, use a spatula to gently lift the edges of the omelet and tilt the pan to allow the uncooked egg to flow underneath. Continue cooking until the eggs are mostly set, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add your desired fillings to one half of the omelet. Use the spatula to gently fold the other half of the omelet over the fillings, forming a half-moon shape.
  5. Cook for an additional minute, or until the fillings are hot and the omelet is fully cooked. Slide the omelet onto a plate and serve hot.
